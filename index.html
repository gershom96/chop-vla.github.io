Abstract

CHOP introduces counterfactual human preference labels to align visuomotor navigation policies with human safety intuition. For each visual observation, the executed trajectory is presented alongside counterfactual alternatives; annotators pick the safer path. Fine-tuning with these preference pairs reduces near-collision events by 49.7%, deviation from human-preferred trajectories by 45.0%, and increases obstacle clearance by 19.8% on SCAND benchmarks. Deployed on a Ghost Robotics Vision60 quadruped, CHOP boosts goal success by 24.4%, raises minimum clearance by 6.8%, cuts collisions/interventions by 45.7%, and improves normalized path completion by 38.6%, while preserving instruction-following performance.

Counterfactual Preference Alignment

CHOP introduces human supervision over counterfactual trajectories so navigation policies can reason about what could happen, not just what was observed.

  • Generate multiple feasible trajectories from the same onboard RGB observation and goal.
  • Collect pairwise human preferences that mark the safer, more socially compliant path.
  • Fine-tune strong visuomotor and VLA-style policies with a preference-based objective to favor human-aligned choices.

This preference signal complements imitation data, encouraging anticipatory obstacle avoidance and smoother path execution without sacrificing goal completion.

Key Results

  • SCAND offline benchmarks: near-collision events ↓ 49.7%, deviation from human-preferred trajectories ↓ 45.0%, average obstacle clearance ↑ 19.8% vs. pretrained baselines.
  • Real-world Ghost Robotics Vision60: goal success ↑ 24.4%, minimum obstacle clearance ↑ 6.8%, collisions/interventions ↓ 45.7%, normalized path completion ↑ 38.6%.
  • Improvements observed across multiple state-of-the-art visuomotor and VLA navigation policies.
  • Safety gains achieved without sacrificing instruction following or task completion.

Data & Experiments

  • SCAND augmented with 1M+ counterfactual human preference pairs over candidate trajectories from identical observations.
  • Counterfactual labels rank alternatives generated from the same RGB frame and goal; executed trajectory is included among options.
  • Evaluations span indoor and outdoor navigation using only onboard RGB at inference; real-world validation on a Vision60 quadruped.
  • Planned release: preference dataset, fine-tuned weights, deployment, and evaluation code.
